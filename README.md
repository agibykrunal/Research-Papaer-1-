---
## Abstract

The release of GPT-4 fundamentally altered the discourse around Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), introducing evidence of broad-domain reasoning, multimodal synthesis, and autonomous cognitive behaviors previously associated with human-level intelligence.
This research investigates whether these capabilities represent genuine progress toward AGI or merely advanced probabilistic pattern matching at scale.

The study combines:

- architectural analysis
- benchmark evaluation
- probabilistic critique
- reasoning framework comparison
- alignment methodology analysis

to construct a comprehensive framework for understanding post-GPT-4 AGI trajectories.
